CHAPTER 11[.

Hannibal learns through his Scouts, that Flaminius is encamped near
Arretium. Marches towards Faesulae. Lays waste with fire and sword
a large tract of fertile Country. Indignation and anger of Flaminius.
Flaminius refusing to listen to prudent counsels, gives the signal for
Marching and Fighting. When mounting his horse, it stumbles and
he is thrown over its head. A standard-hearer, though using his utmost
efforts, unable to pull the Standard from the ground. Principal men
of the Roman army alarmed at these Orneus. The Soldiery rejoice at
the high spirit of their commander.—B.c. 217.
